## 1. Facility Profile

- **Type:** Community-Based Residential Facility (CBRF) — Memory Care only
- **Capacity:** 16 beds; studio and semi-private rooms
- **Served populations:** Residents with irreversible dementia/Alzheimer's disease; HCBS compliant for public funding. Memory care is the facility's *sole* care type — no general assisted living track.
- **Care levels offered:**
  - **Memory Care** — Specialized dementia care; 24-hour supervision; medication management; ADL assistance (bathing, dressing, transfers, incontinence care); non-ambulatory care support; healthcare provider coordination; wheelchair accessible
- **Staffing:** 24/7 coverage with a minimum of 2 awake staff overnight; daytime staff-to-resident ratio of 1:3; on-site registered nurses; staff specially trained in dementia and Alzheimer's care. Family-owned and operated for approximately 25 years under Christine Rindle.
- **Payment:** HCBS compliant — Medicaid Home and Community Based Services waiver eligible. Private pay accepted.
- **Technology:** Emergency alert system; no technology-specific systems described in public listings.
- **Pricing:**

| Room Type | Starting Monthly Cost |
|---|---|
| Semi-Private | ~$5,300 |
| Studio | ~$6,150 |

*Source: A Place for Mom. Seniorly reports starting at $4,490/month; Caring.com lists $4,100/month — figures may reflect different room types or care levels assessed. Dane County area average is approximately $4,363/month; Homestead Living runs above average.*

## 3. What Reviewers Say

**Positives consistently cited:**

- **Staff quality and compassion** — the single most consistent theme across all platforms. Reviewers across every source praise individual caregivers for patience, empathy, and genuine connection with residents.
- **Memory care expertise** — staff are described as well-trained specifically for dementia and Alzheimer's; multiple reviewers noted that caregivers "see the person, not just the disease."
- **Homemade food** — kitchen produces fresh, made-from-scratch meals daily, including homemade pastries and garden-grown produce when seasonally available. Food scores are near-perfect on every platform.
- **Small, home-like scale** — 16-bed size allows highly personalized care; multiple reviewers contrasted Homestead favorably against larger facilities they had previously tried.
- **Activities program** — daily programming led by an activity director: gardening, yoga, art, music therapy, intergenerational visits, pet visits, movie nights, and occasional outings.
- **High staff-to-resident ratio** — daytime 1:3 ratio and minimum 2 awake overnight staff noted specifically as a differentiator.

**Notable positive quotes:**

- *"Staff ratio is excellent, every caregiver understands memory care, creating an environment of love, family and understanding."* — A Place for Mom
- *"My dad lives here now and I can't express how happy my family is that he's getting so much better care."* — A Place for Mom
- *"All staff are excellent with the residents and truly seem to care about their wellbeing."* — Caring.com, Jan 2022
- *"They see beyond her dementia and see her as the person she is inside."* — Caring.com, Jan 2019
- *"More personal feel to it...cleaner...better meals."* — Caring.com, Mar 2019 (reviewer comparing to prior facility)
- *"Phenomenal care, love, and support."* — My Caring Plan
- *"Exceptional kindness, love, compassion, and understanding."* — My Caring Plan

**Concerns and criticisms:**

- **Cleanliness** — one APFM reviewer specifically cited uncleanliness and staff not wearing gloves during meal preparation; Seniorly reviewers mention "outdated and unclean" areas of the physical facility.
- **Morning shift** — at least one review noted disorganization during morning shift transitions specifically.
- **Physical plant** — references to the facility feeling dated; no major renovation or build-out noted in any source.
- **Staffing consistency** — employee reviews (see Section 4) flag turnover and understaffing that could affect continuity of care, though consumer reviews do not echo this concern as frequently.

**Notable critical quotes:**

- *"Not clean or staff did not wear gloves when meal preparing."* — A Place for Mom
- *"Outdated and unclean areas."* — Seniorly (paraphrased from reviewer)
- *"Appears unorganized and having an unmotivated morning staff."* — Seniorly

## 6. Bottom Line

Homestead Living is a memory-care specialist with the strongest combination of documented consumer satisfaction and cost efficiency in this research set: 16 beds, memory care only, independently family-owned for roughly 25 years, an APFM score of 8.9/10, on-site RNs, and pricing ($5,300–$6,150/month) well below the Dane County average. The 1:3 daytime staffing ratio is the most transparent staffing claim of any CBRF in the set, and reviewers consistently compare it favorably to larger facilities they had previously tried. The main concerns are a dated physical plant, isolated reports of cleanliness lapses, and employee reviews that skew negative on management and pay — though the most recent 2025 employee review is positive, suggesting possible improvement. Relative to the neighboring [Hattie's Glen Waunakee](hatties_glen_waunakee.md): Homestead wins on every verifiable quality metric; Hattie's Glen wins on amenities and newer construction but has zero consumer reviews to validate them. Relative to [Brookdale Middleton Stonefield](brookdale_middleton_stonefield.md) — the other established memory-care-only CBRF in the set — Brookdale offers more formal programming structure (Clare Bridge), a more modern building, and on-site RN/LPN coverage comparable to Homestead, but costs $2,000–$2,600/month more. Homestead wins on cost and home-like culture; Brookdale wins on programmatic structure and recent inspection transparency. Among all nine facilities researched, Homestead represents the strongest value proposition for a family seeking dedicated memory care with verified quality and Medicaid eligibility.

**Action item:** Request the DQA survey/inspection history for license 0012266 (email dhswebmaildqa@dhs.wisconsin.gov), ask during a tour what the current caregiver turnover rate is and how many staff are on overnight, and ask to speak with one or two families of current residents before deciding.
