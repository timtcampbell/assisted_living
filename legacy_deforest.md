# The Legacy of DeForest — Research Summary

**Address:** 6639 Pederson Crossing Blvd, DeForest, WI 53532 | **Phone:** (608) 807-2888
**Operator:** Park Vista Senior Housing Management LLC (Park Vista Retirement Living)
**Wisconsin CBRF License:** 0017768 (AL & Memory Care) | **Wisconsin RCAC License:** 0018726 (Independent Living)
**Official website:** [parkvistaliving.org/the-legacy-deforest](https://parkvistaliving.org/the-legacy-deforest/)

> **⚠ REGULATORY FLAG:** The Legacy of DeForest has accumulated **18 deficiencies across 6 inspections since June 2023**, approximately 50% above the Wisconsin statewide average of 12. Fines total approximately **$6,020**. Deficiency patterns include caregiver background check documentation, staff training gaps, fall risk monitoring failures, and failure to notify family representatives after incidents. This is the most significant compliance concern of any facility researched in this project. Consumer reviews are generally positive, creating a gap between reported care experience and regulatory record that warrants careful investigation.

---

## 1. Facility Profile

- **Type:** Community-Based Residential Facility (CBRF, license 0017768) for Assisted Living and Memory Care; Residential Care Apartment Complex (RCAC, license 0018726) for Independent Living
- **Capacity:** 25 beds (CBRF portion); current occupancy 76% (19/25 as of June 2025)
- **Year opened:** 2019 (one of the newer facilities in the area)
- **Served populations:** Advanced aged, terminally ill, individuals with irreversible dementia/Alzheimer's; HCBS compliant for public funding
- **Care levels offered:**
  - **Independent Living** — Apartment-style living with campus amenities; RCAC licensed
  - **Assisted Living** — Customized support for daily living; medication management; 24-hour coverage
  - **Memory Care ("Closer Care")** — Secured area with private rooms and en-suite bathrooms; dementia-specific programming; secure outdoor garden
  - **Respite Care** — Short-term stays available
- **Staffing:** 24/7 coverage; Executive Director, Director of Nursing, Activity Coordinator, Culinary Coordinator (full-time chef), Maintenance Director; staff described as "hand selected." Note: inspection record documents training deficiencies and background check gaps (see Section 5).
- **Payment:** HCBS compliant — both CBRF and RCAC are eligible for Medicaid Home and Community Based Services waivers. Private pay primary. No Medicaid acceptance details disclosed on website.
- **Technology:** **iN2L (It's Never 2 Late)** system — state-of-the-art interactive touchscreen platform offering person-centered programming and meaningful experiences tailored to each resident's history and interests.
- **Pricing:**

| Care Level | Monthly Cost Range | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Assisted Living / Memory Care | $7,910 – $8,910 | Assisted Living Magazine |
| Assisted Living (starting) | $7,250 | Seniorly |

*DeForest-area assisted living average is approximately $3,950/month (Seniorly). The Legacy runs roughly double the local average — comparable to Brookdale Middleton Stonefield in the premium tier.*

- **Amenities:** Library, theater room, on-site pub with happy hour, beauty salon/barber, pet-friendly, transportation services, live entertainment, group activities and outings, secure outdoor garden (memory care), spa room, walking paths.
- **Awards:** "Best of DeForest Windsor 2022 Assisted Living Facility"

## 3. What Reviewers Say

**Positives consistently cited:**

- **Staff warmth** — across all platforms, individual caregivers are described as caring, attentive, and responsive; friendliness scores (4.7/5) are among the highest in the research set.
- **Clean, modern facility** — built in 2019, the building is uniformly praised as "very new," "immaculate," "very spacious," with large private rooms and walk-in showers in the memory care wing.
- **Memory care environment** — separate spa room, singalong activities, secure outdoor garden, and the iN2L system specifically mentioned positively; "the dementia care program" is a common positive theme.
- **Activities** — Activity Coordinator and daily programming praised; iN2L technology enables personalized engagement beyond traditional activity calendars.
- **Housekeeping** — consistently called out positively and separately from general cleanliness.
- **Family communication** — multiple reviewers praise proactive updates; one noted staff "watches calorie intake and provides snacks" as an example of attentiveness.

**Notable positive quotes:**

- *"The Legacy of DeForest had a very warm feeling when you walked in. It was very new. Everything was just immaculate."* — Betty, Caring.com, September 2021
- *"They have great communication and think about situations like a family member does…staff watches calorie intake and provides snacks."* — Cheryl E., A Place for Mom, June 2024
- *"Staff have been great, the housekeeping is good, and the activities are great."* — A Place for Mom reviewer
- *"The staff there was professional looking…very, very spacious private rooms with walk-in showers."* — Caring.com

**Concerns and criticisms:**

- **Food quality** — the single most repeated complaint, appearing in multiple independent reviews. Meals & Dining scores only 3.0/5 on APFM. Specific comments cite poor meal quality and kitchen staff turnover.
- **Staffing shortages** — "can't find enough people to run the place" (APFM, January 2023); kitchen turnover specifically noted.
- **Unfulfilled promises** — one reviewer reported being unable to enroll a relative due to commitments made during the sales process not being honored.
- **Price vs. value tension** — at $7,900–$8,900/month (roughly double the DeForest area average), food and staffing concerns weigh more heavily against cost.

**Notable critical quotes:**

- *"The meals are terrible."* — Anonymous, A Place for Mom, June 2024
- *"They have a staffing issue…can't find enough people to run the place…food is terrible. But…staff are nice and friendly."* — Anonymous, A Place for Mom, January 2023
- *"There has been lots of turnover in the kitchen help."* — A Place for Mom reviewer

## 6. Bottom Line

The Legacy of DeForest presents one of the sharpest contradictions in this research set: the highest consumer-facing score (My Caring Plan state rank 160/1,160 — top 14% in Wisconsin) paired with the most problematic regulatory record of any facility studied. The building is genuinely impressive — new construction, spacious private rooms, iN2L technology, and a full amenity suite — and family reviewers consistently praise staff warmth and the memory care environment. But the DQA inspection record tells a different story: deficiencies found in all six inspections since June 2023, a persistent pattern of staff training gaps, fall risk monitoring failures, and family notification failures, with $6,020 in fines and a verification visit (October 2024) that still found unresolved issues. At $7,900–$8,900/month — double the DeForest area average — families are paying premium prices and should expect a stronger compliance record.

Compared to [Brookdale Middleton Stonefield](brookdale_middleton_stonefield.md): Brookdale had one enforcement action in 2023 that was fully corrected; the Legacy has had deficiencies at every inspection for two years running. Compared to [Homestead Living Waunakee](homestead_living_waunakee.md): Homestead has no found inspection deficiencies and costs roughly half the price. The Legacy's strengths (new building, iN2L, memory care environment, top-ranked in DeForest) are real but do not offset the compliance pattern for families prioritizing regulatory safety.

**Action items:** (1) Read the full inspection reports for both licenses (0017768 and 0018726) via dhswebmaildqa@dhs.wisconsin.gov or the Assisted Living Magazine reports page — specifically the October 2024 verification visit and June 2025 standard survey, which are the most recent. (2) Ask directly: how many caregivers have had their background checks delayed, and what is the current staff training completion rate? (3) Ask what has changed in the care processes since the June 2025 survey to address fall risk assessment and family notification. (4) Request a sample week of menus and, if possible, visit at a mealtime — food is the most consistent family complaint and the meals score (3.0/5) is well below facility averages in every other category.
