## 1. Facility Profile

- **Type:** Community-Based Residential Facility (CBRF) — Memory Care only
- **Capacity:** 32 residents; single-story, circular floor plan designed for resident independence and navigation
- **Served populations:** Individuals with Alzheimer's disease and other forms of dementia (all stages); advanced aged; HCBS compliant for public funding
- **Care levels offered:**
  - **Clare Bridge Memory Care** — Brookdale's signature dementia program, active for 30+ years. Person-centric programming: mental workouts, gentle movement, sensory therapy, pet visits, neighborhood walks, crafts, music, and hymn singing. Individualized approach calibrated to each resident's history and preferences.
  - **Clare Bridge Solace** — Advanced-stage dementia track emphasizing comfort, sensory engagement, and dignity for residents who are less cognitively and physically responsive.
- **Staffing:** 24/7 trained staff on-site; RN or LPN on staff; nurse on call around the clock; fully awake night-shift team. Keypad entry for resident security.
- **Payment:** Private pay; VA benefits accepted; HCBS compliant (Medicaid waiver eligible). Brookdale's website references financial planning resources but does not list Medicaid acceptance — verify current HCBS waiver eligibility directly.
- **Technology:** Emergency alert system; keypad entry; Brookdale's "Personal Solutions" discreet home-delivery program for toiletries and hygiene supplies.
- **Pricing:**

|---|---|---|
| Studio (memory care) | $7,810 | Brookdale official website, 05/14/2026 |
| Studio (memory care) | $7,420 | Caring.com |

*Dane County area average for assisted living / memory care is approximately $5,200–$5,600/month. Brookdale Middleton Stonefield runs 35–50% above the local average, consistent with the Brookdale national brand positioning.*

- **Amenities:** Greenhouse/gardening, woodworking shop, fireside living room, library, beauty/barber salon, secured outdoor patio, walking paths, piano, postal services, WiFi, cable TV, transportation assistance, on-site café.

## 3. What Reviewers Say

**Positives consistently cited:**

- **Staff quality** — the dominant theme across all platforms. Staff receive near-universal praise for attentiveness, kindness, and dementia-specific expertise. The 4.6–4.7 staff scores on APFM and Caring.com are among the highest of any facility in this research set.
- **Small, personalized scale** — 32 residents means staff learn individual histories and preferences; families consistently cite this as what "sold" them on the facility.
- **Circular single-story layout** — designed specifically for memory care; allows residents to walk independently without dead ends or confusion. Specifically praised by multiple reviewers.
- **Clare Bridge programming** — activities tailored to each resident's past interests and cognitive level; greenhouse, woodworking, music, and sensory programs repeatedly cited as meaningful.
- **Family communication** — multiple reviewers commend proactive outreach when health changes occur; seen as a differentiator from larger facilities.
- **Physical environment** — greenhouse, secured outdoor patio, fireside living room, and woodworking shop cited as warm and purposeful spaces.

**Notable positive quotes:**

- *"Staff are consistently attentive and kind. The small facility convinced us we found the best option for her."* — Mike K., A Place for Mom
- *"I have over 30 years of healthcare experience and I'm impressed by the skill, expertise, and love shown to my mom."* — Rhonda G., A Place for Mom
- *"The facility has more than met my expectations. I have watched my friend blossom here."* — Lauri Roman (Guardian), A Place for Mom
- *"I have never seen such a connected group of families. The staff there are always happy and engaging with everyone."* — RuthAnne S., Caring.com, May 2021
- *"The caregivers are top notch and absolutely do care about the residents. It's not fancy, but it is amazing."* — Lynn H., Caring.com, Feb 2019
- *"The residents seemed quite happy, they were occupied, and the staff were busy."* — Dottie, Caring.com, Sept 2025

**Concerns and criticisms:**

- **Weekend staffing** — the most frequently cited operational concern: reduced staffing on weekends leaves gaps in coverage and responsiveness.
- **Corporate profit focus** — recurring criticism that Brookdale's corporate leadership prioritizes revenue over resident welfare; mentioned by multiple independent reviewers across years.
- **Value for cost** — lowest category score (3.9/5 on APFM); at $7,400–$7,800/month, families expect more consistent staffing levels.
- **Historical management issues** — a 2019 review specifically named an executive director as "uncaring and incompetent"; management churn is a Brookdale chain-wide issue. Less evident in recent reviews.
- **Historical food quality** — pre-2020 reviews mention food concerns that do not appear in recent reviews; improvement likely under current kitchen management.
- **Resident belongings** — isolated reports of missing or neglected personal belongings.

**Notable critical quotes:**

- *"They are friendly, but like most facilities, they are short of help, especially on the weekends."* — Anonymous, A Place for Mom
- *"The management is terrible. The executive director is uncaring and incompetent."* — Annie Potter, Caring.com, Jan 2019
- *"The management at the facility and at the corporate level was really terrible. They care only about profit, not about the well-being of the residents."* — Caring.com reviewer
- *"I didn't see anyone washing hands or wearing gloves."* — Watch Dog for Elderly, Caring.com, Feb 2017
- *"Clare Bridge is part of Brookdale Senior Living, an out-of-state corporation."* — suestoo, Caring.com, Aug 2011 (noting corporate chain concern)

## 6. Bottom Line

Brookdale Middleton Stonefield is the most professionally structured and programmatically mature memory care option among the facilities researched in this project. Its 32-bed size, circular layout, Clare Bridge / Solace programs, and on-site RN/LPN coverage are genuine differentiators — and the APFM reviews (47 reviews, 9.5/10, 58% five-star) are the strongest consumer signal of any facility researched to date. The significant caveat is cost: at $7,400–$7,800/month, it runs 35–50% above the Dane County memory care average, and the low Value for Cost score (3.9/5) reflects that gap. The 2023 enforcement action — medication administration and blood sugar monitoring failures in a memory care unit — is serious and should be read in full before making a decision, though the fact that all deficiencies were corrected and the 2025 inspection was clean suggests a responsive management response. Weekend staffing shortages, mentioned by multiple independent reviewers over several years, is the operational concern that the 2023 deficiency history makes harder to dismiss.

Compared to [Homestead Living Waunakee](homestead_living_waunakee.md), which is also memory-care only: Brookdale wins on structural transparency (RN on staff, formal programs, inspection records available), Homestead wins on cost ($5,300–$6,150 vs. $7,400–$7,800) and a more family-owned culture with stronger recent staff praise. Compared to [Sage Meadow Middleton](sage_meadow_middleton.md), Brookdale has substantially stronger consumer reviews, a cleaner operator history, and much more verifiable data.

**Action items:** (1) Read the full August 2023 DQA survey report (license 110304, available via dhswebmaildqa@dhs.wisconsin.gov or the Assisted Living Magazine reports page) to understand the specific medication and blood sugar monitoring deficiencies. (2) Ask during a tour what the current weekend staffing model is and how many caregivers are on shift on Saturday and Sunday nights specifically. (3) Clarify current HCBS waiver acceptance — the DQA lists HCBS compliance but Brookdale's website focuses on private pay and does not prominently feature Medicaid acceptance.
