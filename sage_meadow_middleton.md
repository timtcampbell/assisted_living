# Sage Meadow Middleton — Research Summary

**Address:** 5330 Century Ave, Middleton, WI 53562 | **Phone:** (608) 888-8330
**Operator:** Koru Health LLC
**Wisconsin CBRF License:** 0019367
**Official website:** [sagemeadowmiddleton.com](https://www.sagemeadowmiddleton.com/)

> **⚠ CAMPUS COMPLEXITY — READ FIRST:** The Century Ave campus in Middleton has two adjacent, separately licensed CBRF buildings. The 5330 Century Ave building (license 0019367, Koru Health LLC, licensed May 2023) is the current "Sage Meadow Middleton." The adjacent 5340 Century Ave building (license 0019366) appears to have been rebranded as **Hatties Bay Middleton** by NuCare Senior Living — the same operator as Hattie's Glen Waunakee. The A Place for Mom listing formerly titled "Sage Meadow Middleton" (community ID 87535) now displays Hatties Bay Middleton data. Online review databases aggregate reviews across multiple operators and both buildings under the "Sage Meadow" name, so historical reviews reflect the 5340 building under prior operators. Verify which building and which operator you are scheduling a tour with before drawing conclusions from any single rating source.

## 1. Facility Profile

- **Type:** Community-Based Residential Facility (CBRF) — Memory Care (primary) with Assisted Living and Respite Care
- **Capacity:** 52 licensed beds; all 52 designated for Alzheimer's/dementia care (license 0019367)
- **Served populations:** Advanced aged, individuals with irreversible dementia/Alzheimer's, physically disabled, developmentally disabled; HCBS compliant for public funding
- **Care levels offered:**
  - **Memory Care** — Full dementia/Alzheimer's care program; 24-hour supervision; medication management; ADL assistance
  - **Assisted Living** — Customized support "as little or as much help as desired"; personalized care plans
  - **Respite Care** — Short-term stays available
- **Staffing:** 24/7 caregiver coverage; Activity Director on staff; specific staff-to-resident ratios not publicly disclosed. The official website notes "little turnover" in resident testimonials, though employee reviews partially contradict this.
- **Payment:** HCBS compliant — Medicaid Home and Community Based Services waiver eligible. Financial assistance mentioned on official website for qualifying residents. Other payment details not specified publicly.
- **Technology:** Emergency alert system mentioned; no additional technology-specific systems described.
- **Pricing:**

| Care Level | Starting Monthly Cost | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Assisted Living / Memory Care | ~$5,330 | Caring.com |
| Range | $4,733–$7,500+ | Multiple directories |

*Area average for Middleton assisted living is approximately $5,200–$5,600/month. Sage Meadow is within or near this range depending on room type and assessed care level.*

- **Setting:** Located near Pheasant Branch Conservancy and Lake Mendota; enclosed courtyard, balconies with gardening opportunities, scheduled transportation.

## 3. What Reviewers Say

**Positives consistently cited:**

- **Staff compassion** — multiple reviewers describe individual caregivers as kind, engaged, and genuinely invested in residents; "bend over backwards" appears across platforms.
- **Homelike atmosphere** — smaller scale and communal living rooms described as warm; residents feel part of a community rather than an institution.
- **End-of-life / hospice care** — specifically praised by one family for "exemplary end of life care" and "loving and tender care."
- **Improvement under management changes** — several reviews note that a responsive director improved communication and care delivery; suggests positive trajectory under newer leadership.
- **Physical environment** — common spaces described as "homey and clean"; arts, crafts, movies, and activity programming noted positively.

**Notable positive quotes:**

- *"Loving and tender care…exemplary end of life care for my mother."* — Birdeye (Google review, Kathy Gunta)
- *"Staff members bend over backwards and are very helpful and kind."* — Birdeye (Google review, Pam Williams)
- *"The staff members are a 5 plus, they are so friendly."* — A Place for Mom, 2021
- *"Very impressed by the staff. Everyone that I have met so far has been very polite."* — Caring.com, January 2020
- *"Clean, the staff members were courteous and seemed engaged."* — Caring.com, March 2022
- *"Homey and clean"* with arts, crafts, and movies — Caring.com, February 2018

**Concerns and criticisms:**

- **Staffing and management churn** — the most consistent negative theme across all platforms: frequent changes in executive directors, nursing leadership, and frontline staff disrupt continuity.
- **Care lapses** — some reviewers describe residents left in dirty conditions, medications administered incorrectly or without proper orders, and safety issues promised but not resolved.
- **Physical plant** — carpet described as soiled with burn marks; general wear-and-tear noted.
- **Communication** — poor internal communication between shifts; some families report difficulty reaching staff by phone.
- **Ownership/operator changes** — multiple reviewers call out the churn of operators as undermining the quality of care over time.

**Notable critical quotes:**

- *"Sage Meadow is going through a lot of staff changes, from executive directors to nurses in charge of medications."* — review (multiple platforms)
- *"A nightmare…frequent ownership changes and concerning employee conduct."* — Birdeye (Google review, Richard Christiansen)
- *"Manager has a very nasty attitude and poor job performance."* — Birdeye (Google review, Rolanda Scott)
- *"Care is horrible, staff sleeps in cars and on couches…staff on phones and social media all times of the day"* — A Place for Mom, 2024 *(attributed to the 5340 Century Ave building, likely now Hatties Bay Middleton under NuCare)*
- *"Carpeting that was soiled with burn marks…safety issues that were not resolved as promised."* — multiple platforms

## 6. Bottom Line

Sage Meadow Middleton sits in an operationally complex position: it is a newer licensed building (Koru Health LLC, 2023) on a campus where the adjacent facility has already been sold to and rebranded by NuCare. The brand-level reviews accumulated over years under multiple operators — including "The Artisan" and at least one prior Sage Meadow management team — make it very difficult to assess the current Koru Health operation on its own merits, and the two available Middleton-specific employee reviews are both negative. The campus's instability (frequent director and ownership changes cited across many reviews) is the clearest through-line in the data. That said, family reviewers consistently praise individual caregivers and the physical environment, and some families have reported significant improvement under newer management — suggesting the care floor is decent even when institutional leadership is volatile.

Compared to [Homestead Living Waunakee](homestead_living_waunakee.md) — which is also memory-care focused and of similar size — Sage Meadow has a weaker track record of management stability and lower statewide ranking (766 vs. 692 in Wisconsin), though Sage Meadow's 52-bed campus is larger and offers more amenity variety. Compared to [Heritage Middleton](heritage_middleton.md) and [Hattie's Glen Waunakee](hatties_glen_waunakee.md), Sage Meadow's mixed operator history is a meaningful disadvantage.

**Action items before any tour:** (1) Confirm with the facility directly which building you are touring (5330 or 5340 Century Ave) and whether Koru Health LLC is the current licensed operator — the Koru Health corporate website no longer lists Middleton as an active location, which is a flag requiring direct verification. (2) Request the DQA survey history for both license numbers (0019367 and 0019366) via dhswebmaildqa@dhs.wisconsin.gov. (3) Ask specifically how long the current executive director and director of nursing have been in their roles, given the documented turnover.
