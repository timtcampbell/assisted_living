# Complete Care at Maple Grove — Research Summary

**Address:** 3401 Maple Grove Drive, Madison, WI 53719 | **Phone:** (608) 845-1000
**Operator:** Complete Care Management (legal entity: PC WCM Opco Holdco LLC; ownership chain: PC WCM Topco LLC → Peace Capital Holdings II LLC → SMS 2021 Trust; corporate officer: Shalom Stein)
**CMS Certification Number (CCN):** 525276
**Legal business name:** St Marys Care and Rehab Center LLC
**Official website:** [ccmaplegrove.com](https://ccmaplegrove.com)

> ⚠ **Facility type — important:** Complete Care at Maple Grove is a **skilled nursing facility (nursing home)**, not an assisted living (CBRF/RCAC) community. It is federally regulated and certified under Medicare and Medicaid, which means it serves significantly higher-acuity residents than CBRFs in this research set, follows federal CMS inspection standards rather than Wisconsin DHS CBRF rules, and appears in the CMS Care Compare database. If the person being placed requires only assisted living or memory care, this facility may be clinically and financially above what is needed. Review this report with that distinction in mind.
>
> ⚠ **Ownership change:** The facility was formerly **SSM Health St. Mary's Care Center** (non-profit Catholic health system) for many years. SSM Health announced the sale to Complete Care (a for-profit company headquartered in New Jersey) in July 2024. Wisconsin's DHS **initially blocked the sale** citing Complete Care's "history of noncompliance." After an appeal, the parties reached a settlement and the transfer proceeded under a **probationary license with conditions**. The facility now operates as Complete Care at Maple Grove.

## 1. Facility Profile

- **Type:** Skilled Nursing Facility (SNF) / Nursing Home — Medicare and Medicaid certified. NOT a CBRF or RCAC.
- **Capacity:** 184 certified beds; average daily census approximately 95–106 residents
- **Served populations:** Post-acute rehabilitation (short-term), long-term skilled nursing care, Alzheimer's/dementia/memory care, non-ambulatory residents, medically complex residents (dialysis, cardiac, wound care, ventilator support per Complete Care's corporate capabilities list)
- **Care levels offered:**
  - **Post-Acute Rehabilitation** — Short-term skilled nursing and physical/occupational/speech therapy following hospitalization, surgery, or acute illness
  - **Long-Term Care** — Ongoing skilled nursing for residents who cannot safely return home
  - **Memory Care** — Specialized care for Alzheimer's and related dementia diagnoses within the nursing home setting
- **Physical plant:** Designed as a "household" model with approximately 12 residents per unit; private rooms with personal bathrooms; outdoor patios and gardens; restaurant-style dining; beauty salon; chapel; media/activities room (from prior SSM Health description — NuCare has not published updated building details)
- **Staffing:**
  - Nurse hours per resident per day: **4.93** (Wisconsin state average: 4.2) — above average
  - Nurse aide hours per resident per day: 2.99
  - Physical therapist hours per resident per day: 0.03
  - **Nurse turnover rate: 89.1%** (Wisconsin state average: 47.4%) — nearly double the state average; the single most alarming operational metric at this facility
- **Payment:** Medicare (Yes), Medicaid (Yes), private pay accepted
- **CMS certification:** Active since January 1, 1977 (under prior SSM Health / St. Mary's name); transferred to Complete Care LLC 2024
- **Pricing:**

**Note on the staffing paradox:** The 4-star staffing rating reflects nurse hours per resident per day (4.93 vs. state average 4.2 — above average on paper), yet the nurse turnover rate of 89.1% — nearly double the state average of 47.4% — suggests chronic instability in who those staff are. This combination correlates with care continuity failures.

## 3. What Reviewers Say

**Consumer review context:** Independent consumer reviews are extremely limited for this facility, particularly under the Complete Care name. Most online reviews still appear under "St. Mary's Care Center." The facility's own website (ccmaplegrove.com) features curated testimonials; these are not independently verified.

**Positives (primarily from facility-curated sources and 2020-era SSM Health reviews):**

- **Physical environment** — multiple reviewers describe the building as calm, quiet, clean, and odor-free; private rooms with private bathrooms are noted as a significant differentiator from older facilities
- **Staff interactions** — individual staff members described as friendly, respectful, and accommodating in adjusting care plans to day-to-day needs
- **Food** — food choices described as satisfying in some older reviews
- **Activity programming** — described as keeping residents "stimulated" in older reviews

**Notable positive quotes (from curated or older sources — treat with appropriate caution):**

- *"The environment is calm, quiet, clean, and odor free. The staff are friendly and accommodating, ready to adjust the care plan based on her needs that vary from day-to-day."* — facility testimonials page
- *"They take great care of her! The staff has been very understanding and helpful with all of my mom's needs."* — facility testimonials page

**Concerns and criticisms:**

- **Two Immediate Jeopardy incidents (2023–2024)** — the most severe CMS classification, meaning surveyors found that the situation caused or was likely to cause serious injury, harm, impairment, or death:
  - *Incident 1 (December 4, 2023):* A 106-year-old resident with Alzheimer's disease died after a nursing assistant removed her fall mat and turned her back on her; the resident rolled out of bed and struck her head on the floor
  - *Incident 2 (September 2024):* A man with Parkinson's disease and documented swallowing difficulties was given a piece of melon in violation of his care plan — he required hospitalization; the same resident had been hospitalized for choking on a hot dog in July 2024, also a care plan violation
- **Substandard Quality of Care** — the facility was formally cited for this designation in both the 2023–2024 survey cycle and again in the 2024 survey; SQC is a threshold finding that triggers extended survey authority
- **Deficiency rates more than double state average** — 21 health citations vs. Wisconsin average of 10.2; 14 fire safety citations vs. Wisconsin average of 7.1
- **Nurse turnover nearly double state average** — 89.1% vs. 47.4% statewide; a direct indicator of care continuity risk
- **38 deficiencies in 2024 alone** — against a state average of 13.0 for similar-sized facilities

**Notable critical information:**

- *"History of noncompliance with regulations"* — Wisconsin DHS's stated reason for initially blocking Complete Care's acquisition of this facility (from published news coverage)
- CMS triggered a Denial of Payment for New Admissions Dec 30, 2023–Feb 15, 2024 — meaning no new Medicare/Medicaid residents could be admitted for 47 days
- Nurse Aide Training and Competency Evaluation Programs (NATCEP) prohibited at this facility through December 4, 2025, due to the Substandard Quality of Care finding

## 6. Bottom Line

Complete Care at Maple Grove carries the worst regulatory profile of any facility in this research set — and it is a different category of care entirely. As a Medicare/Medicaid-certified skilled nursing facility (not an assisted living CBRF), it serves higher-acuity residents and carries federal oversight obligations that most families will encounter for the first time during a placement decision. Its 1-star overall CMS rating, two Immediate Jeopardy citations within a year (one resulting in a resident death), 38 deficiencies in 2024 against a state average of 13, 89.1% nurse turnover, and Wisconsin's own DHS blocking its new owner's application for regulatory history are a convergence of red flags that is difficult to reconcile with any placement recommendation under current conditions. The above-average nurse hours per resident per day (4.93 vs. 4.2 state average) are a genuine positive, but they have not translated into safe outcomes — the IJ incidents and SQC findings involve care planning, supervision, and safety protocol failures rather than understaffing per se.

**Action item:** Before any consideration of this facility, pull the current CMS Care Compare profile at medicare.gov to read the actual Survey Reports (CMS-2567 forms) with detailed deficiency narratives — these are public documents, free to access, and far more informative than summary scores. Contact the Wisconsin LTC Ombudsman Program at (800) 815-0015 for a free, independent review of the facility's complaint and inspection history. If a skilled nursing level of care is genuinely required, compare against other Madison-area SNFs with higher CMS star ratings before deciding. If the person being placed may qualify for assisted living (CBRF), the other facilities in this research set are in a fundamentally different regulatory category with substantially lower deficiency counts.
