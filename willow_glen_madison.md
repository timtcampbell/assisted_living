# Willow Glen — Research Summary

**Address:** 5555 Burke Road (Oakwood), 5565 Burke Rd (Applewood), 5575 Burke Rd (Elmwood), Madison, WI 53718 | **Phone:** (608) 620-7474
**Operator:** NuCare Senior Living LLC (rebranded from NOHC Cottages of Madison LLC; formerly operated independently as *Cottages of Madison* for 20+ years)
**Wisconsin CBRF Licenses:** See Section 5 — three buildings, multiple license numbers (transition in progress)
**Official website:** [willowglen-wi.com](https://www.willowglen-wi.com)

> ⚠ **Name and operator history:** This facility operated as **Cottages of Madison** (three ranch-style cottages — Applewood, Elmwood, Oakwood) for over 20 years before NuCare Senior Living LLC assumed management. The facility now markets under the "Willow Glen" brand but appears in regulatory and many third-party databases as "Cottages of Madison." Reviews span multiple eras of management. See Sections 3 and 5 for how this affects interpretation.

---

## 1. Facility Profile

- **Type:** Community-Based Residential Facility (CBRF) — Assisted Living and Memory Care
- **Capacity:** 48 private rooms total across 3 separate ranch-style cottage buildings, each licensed for 16 residents
  - **Oakwood:** 5555 Burke Rd — the primary public-facing address
  - **Applewood:** 5565 Burke Rd
  - **Elmwood:** 5575 Burke Rd
- **Served populations:** Advanced aged; irreversible dementia/Alzheimer's; physically disabled; HCBS compliant for public funding. All three buildings are licensed for Alzheimer's/memory care.
- **Care levels offered:**
  - **Assisted Living** — 24-hour on-site staff; medication management; ADL assistance (bathing, dressing, transfers, incontinence care); call alarm systems and personal alarm pendants; housekeeping, laundry, and meal preparation
  - **Memory Care** — All AL services plus secured outdoor spaces and memory-care-trained staff
  - **Respite Care** — Short-term stays available
- **Staffing:** 24/7 on-site staff with "low caregiver-to-resident ratios" per facility marketing. No specific daytime ratio or licensed RN on-site requirement is publicly disclosed. Facility added an activities coordinator and nursing staff following documented care concerns in 2020–2021 (per facility response to a Caring.com review). Employee reviews (see Section 4) raise ongoing concerns about staffing turnover.
- **Payment:** HCBS compliant — Medicaid Home and Community Based Services waiver eligible. VA coordination cited in at least one resident review. Private pay accepted.
- **Technology:** Personal alarm pendant call system; secured outdoor areas.
- **Pricing:**

| Care Level | Starting Monthly Cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Assisted Living | ~$3,000–$3,700 | Studio; Seniorly range $2,930–$4,900 |
| Memory Care | ~$3,700–$5,000 | APFM lists $3,700 starting; Caring.com and SeniorHomes list $5,000 |

*Madison area average for assisted living: ~$4,310–$5,335/month. Willow Glen's AL pricing is notably below area average, which may reflect smaller room size, lower amenity level, or pricing strategy under the NuCare rebrand.*

## 3. What Reviewers Say

**Important context:** Reviews span at least three phases of management history:
- **Pre-2021 (prior management):** Serious documented neglect concerns
- **2020–2024 (early NuCare transition):** Mixed — improvements claimed but enforcement action still issued in Oct 2024
- **2025–2026 (current):** Recent consumer reviews are notably positive, crediting "new administration"

**Positives consistently cited (2025–2026 reviews):**

- **Management turnaround** — three of the six APFM reviews specifically credit a change in administration for improvements; this is the single most prominent theme in recent feedback
- **Staff attentiveness** — current-era reviews describe staff as caring, friendly, and attentive
- **VA coordination** — one reviewer specifically praised the facility's coordination with Veterans Affairs benefits
- **Small cottage scale** — the 16-resident-per-building format creates a home-like setting; multiple reviewers find the scale a strength

**Notable positive quotes:**

- *"New administration improved facility condition. Mom feels safe and happy."* — A Place for Mom, April 2026 (Hiram Rubio)
- *"Staff provided excellent care, though administrative responsiveness was lacking."* — A Place for Mom, March 2026
- *"New management transformed the place — significant improvements noticed."* — A Place for Mom, June 2025 (D. Shephard)
- *"Staff caring and attentive. Leadership excellent. Great VA coordination."* — A Place for Mom, Feb 2020

**Concerns and criticisms:**

- **Documented neglect (2020–2021)** — the most serious concern in this research set: a resident was sent to the ER with bedsores and dehydration; family reported staff was "mean," that medications were withheld, and that privacy locks on doors were turned around to lock residents *inside* their rooms. Reviewer explicitly called care "neglectful and abusive."
- **No call buttons at that time** — 2020–2021 reviewers noted the absence of a pendant call system; the facility claims this was added after complaints
- **Medication management failures** — multiple 2020–2021 reviews cited residents not receiving prescribed medications
- **No activities** — 2020–2021 era reviewers reported no structured programming; facility claims activities coordinator was added
- **Administrative responsiveness** — a 2026 reviewer still noted administrative responsiveness was "lacking" despite praising staff care
- **Physical plant** — one employee review (2026) describes the facility as "filthy"; no renovation or build-out noted in any source

**Notable critical quotes:**

- *"Care was neglectful; patient had bedsores, dehydration, medication issues."* — A Place for Mom, January 2021 (Stanley Livingston)
- *"I recommend staying away from this facility at least until county inspections find that The Cottages are meeting minimum standards."* — Third-party review source
- *"The place is filthy...pay is very low...office staff is lazy."* — Indeed employee review, 2026

**Facility's own response (on file at Caring.com):** The facility responded to the 2021 complaint review acknowledging past shortcomings and claiming improvements: installation of a pendant call system, hiring an activities coordinator for all three buildings, adding nursing staff, and implementing minimum staffing requirements. Consumer reviews from 2025–2026 appear to support a partial turnaround, but the Oct 2024 enforcement action (see Section 5) indicates regulatory issues persisted after these changes were purportedly made.

These employee ratings should be weighted heavily against the recently improving consumer scores.

## 6. Bottom Line

Willow Glen has the most complex history of any CBRF in this research set. It operated for over 20 years as Cottages of Madison before NuCare Senior Living took over, and serious documented care failures — bedsores, dehydration, withheld medications, and locked residents — are on the record from 2020–2021. Consumer reviews from 2025–2026 are notably positive and consistently credit a management turnaround, which is encouraging. However, the October 2024 DQA enforcement action (abuse investigation failures and notification failures) shows that regulatory problems persisted several years into NuCare's tenure, and the employee satisfaction profile is the worst among CBRF facilities in this research set: 1.6/5 overall with management and pay both at 1.0/5 as recently as 2026. Among CBRFs, the nearest regulatory analog is [Legacy of DeForest](legacy_deforest.md), which also has documented DQA deficiencies across multiple survey cycles — though Legacy's are primarily operational and documentation failures, not direct neglect of residents. For scale context, [Complete Care at Maple Grove](complete_care_maple_grove.md) — the only nursing home in the full research set — carries Immediate Jeopardy citations and a resident death: a severity tier above anything documented in the CBRF set, and a different regulatory category entirely. The below-area-average pricing for AL raises a question about whether care investment is keeping pace with the marketing rebrand. Families should treat the positive recent reviews as a signal worth investigating — not a conclusion.

**Action item:** Before any placement decision, request the full DQA inspection history for all six license numbers (0017702, 0017703, and presumably the third original number for Elmwood, plus 0021171, 0021188, 0021203) via dhswebmaildqa@dhs.wisconsin.gov. During a tour, ask specifically: what is the current staff turnover rate, how many caregivers are on overnight, is there a licensed nurse on-call or on-site, and what the current caregiver-to-resident ratio is. Ask to speak with at least two families of current residents — given the management-change narrative, specifically seek out families whose loved ones have been there at least 12 months.
