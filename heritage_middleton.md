# Heritage Middleton — Research Summary

## Facility Profile

- **Type:** Residential Care Apartment Complex (RCAC) with CBRF-licensed Enhanced Assisted Living and Memory Care wings
- **Capacity:** 79 units — studios, 1-bedroom, and 2-bedroom options
- **Location:** Next to Lake Mendota in Dane County
- **Care levels offered:**
  - **Assisted Living** — up to 28 hours/week of personalized care
  - **Enhanced Assisted Living (CBRF)** — for residents needing more physical or medical assistance
  - **Memory Care** — 24/7 specialized care for Alzheimer's and related dementias
- **Staffing:** 24/7 staff; full-time licensed nurse on-site; higher staff-to-resident ratio in Enhanced AL
- **Payment:** HCBS-compliant for public funding; Medicaid accepted once private funds are exhausted; long-term care insurance accepted
- **Technology:** Vayyar Care fall-detection system; TrueLoo smart bathroom health monitoring

## What Reviewers Say

**Positives consistently cited:**
- Clean, attractive facility with well-maintained common areas
- Staff generally described as friendly, jovial, and caring on an individual level
- Broad amenity set: rehabilitation gym, salon, pub area, outdoor spaces, movie nights, library, walking paths
- On-site therapy services
- Engaging activities calendar; residents report making lasting friendships
- Accepts Medicaid (once private funds are exhausted) — noted positively by families planning long-term
- Full-time licensed nurse on-site (unlike some smaller competitors)

**Notable positive quotes:**
- *"Heritage Middleton is an awesome facility, it is very clean and beautiful with very nice people."* — Birdeye reviewer
- *"My mom has been in 2 other care facilities and this one is top notch! She feels at home here."* — Caring.com reviewer (Jan 2022)
- *"Heritage Middleton is a beautiful place...The staff who assisted us was very nice and very informative."* — Caring.com reviewer (Sept 2022)
- *"The staff is very jovial and fun...for the most part they do a great job."* — A Place for Mom reviewer

**Concerns and criticisms:**
- **High staff turnover** cited repeatedly as a driver of inconsistent care
- **Understaffing** — multiple reviews mention call-light response times and insufficient aides per shift
- **Medication management issues** — specific complaints about errors and lapses
- **Housekeeping** — light cleaning described as inadequate; mirrors, dusting, and sheet changes skipped
- **Food quality** — lowest-rated category (2.5/5 on Caring.com); limited substitution options for dietary preferences
- **Memory care concerns** — reports of neglect and inadequate staff training specific to dementia unit
- **Management** — described as a "toxic work culture" in employee reviews; management changes cited as driving quality decline
- **Safety incident** — at least one reviewer cited a fall resulting in a concussion
- **Legal/financial dispute** — one reviewer on Caring.com alleged eviction of an 87-year-old after funds were depleted despite contractual assurances

**Notable critical quotes:**
- *"The cleaning service does no dusting, no cleaning mirrors, no bed sheet changing."* — Birdeye reviewer
- *"Reports of neglect, inadequate staff training, and management issues"* particularly in Memory Care — Seniorly summary
- *"They evicted our 87-year-old friend after taking her life savings in exchange for their promise."* — Caring.com reviewer (July 2019)

## State Regulatory / Inspection Data

- **Licensed by:** Wisconsin Department of Health Services, Division of Quality Assurance
  - RCAC License: **0014201** (6234 Maywood Ave location)
  - CBRF license applies to Enhanced Assisted Living and Memory Care wings
- **HCBS compliant:** Yes — eligible for public funding/Medicaid waiver residents
- **Inspections:** No specific violations or citations were surfaced in public searches. Wisconsin DHS inspection records are accessible via the [ForwardHealth DQA Provider Search](https://www.forwardhealth.wi.gov/WIPortal/Subsystem/Public/DQAProviderSearch.aspx) (being replaced by Wisconsin Provider Finder in spring 2026). Statements of deficiency can be requested by emailing dhswebmaildqa@dhs.wisconsin.gov.
- Unlike Beehive Mt. Horeb, Heritage Middleton accepts Medicaid and is HCBS-compliant, making it subject to broader state oversight and potentially visible in CMS systems for its CBRF-licensed components.

## Bottom Line

Heritage Middleton is the only full-continuum campus in this research set — independent through memory care, 79 units, on-site licensed nurse, and Medicaid acceptance — and the natural choice for families who need that range under one roof. However, its consumer reviews are the lowest of any CBRF in the research set: 6.1/10 on A Place for Mom drawn from 79 reviews, which is the largest and most statistically reliable review sample of any facility studied. That combination — most reviews, lowest score — is a more meaningful signal than the handful of reviews behind higher-scoring facilities. Recurring complaints about staff turnover, housekeeping, food quality, and memory care management are consistent enough across 79 reviews and multiple platforms to treat as structural rather than isolated. The physical facility receives consistent praise; care delivery is where reviews diverge. For families whose situation doesn't require the full IL-through-memory-care continuum: [Brookdale Middleton Stonefield](brookdale_middleton_stonefield.md) offers a more specialized, better-reviewed memory care option at a comparable or higher price in the same city; [Beehive Mt. Horeb](beehive_mt_horeb.md) is the cleanest regulatory and consumer record in the set at lower cost; and [Homestead Living Waunakee](homestead_living_waunakee.md) offers a comparable on-site-nurse, HCBS-compliant memory care option with stronger recent staff reviews. Families considering Heritage should ask specifically about current staffing ratios, memory care protocols, and housekeeping standards — and request state inspection records for the CBRF-licensed wings.
