## Facility Type Note

The eight CBRFs in this set (Wisconsin Community-Based Residential Facilities) are regulated by the Wisconsin DHS Division of Quality Assurance. They serve residents who need assistance with daily living, memory care, or mild to moderate medical needs. They do **not** appear in the federal CMS Care Compare database.

Complete Care at Maple Grove is a **skilled nursing facility (nursing home)** regulated under federal CMS standards and certified for Medicare and Medicaid. It serves residents with significantly higher clinical needs (post-acute rehab, medically complex, ventilator-dependent). Its CMS inspection methodology and deficiency framework are not directly comparable to Wisconsin CBRF inspections. Families deciding between the two care levels should consult with a physician, geriatric care manager, or hospital discharge planner about whether AL or skilled nursing is clinically appropriate.

## Glossary of Acronyms

### Facility & License Types

| Acronym | Full Name | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **CBRF** | Community-Based Residential Facility | Wisconsin license type; 5–20 beds; regulated by Wisconsin DHS/DQA; does not appear in federal CMS Care Compare |
| **RCAC** | Residential Care Apartment Complex | Wisconsin license type; larger, apartment-style; HCBS-eligible; regulated by DQA |
| **SNF** | Skilled Nursing Facility | Federally regulated nursing home; Medicare/Medicaid certified; appears in CMS Care Compare; clinically distinct from CBRFs |
| **AFH** | Adult Family Home | Wisconsin license type; 1–4 residents; not represented in this research set |

### Care Levels

| Acronym | Full Name | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **IL** | Independent Living | Minimal or no personal care assistance; residents manage most daily tasks independently |
| **AL** | Assisted Living | Structured support for daily activities (bathing, dressing, meals, medication management) |
| **EAL** | Enhanced Assisted Living | Higher-acuity AL; more intensive personal care, often for residents not ambulatory or with significant medical needs; CBRF-licensed at Heritage Middleton |
| **MC** | Memory Care | Specialized care for Alzheimer's disease and related dementias; secured environments, dementia-trained staff, structured programming |
| **LTC** | Long-Term Care | Ongoing skilled nursing care for residents who cannot return home; used within SNF context |

### Staffing & Nursing

| Acronym | Full Name | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **RN** | Registered Nurse | Highest nursing credential; can assess, diagnose nursing problems, and delegate care |
| **LPN** | Licensed Practical Nurse | Mid-level nursing credential; works under RN or physician supervision |
| **LN** | Licensed Nurse | General term covering both RN and LPN |
| **FT** | Full-Time | As in "full-time LN on-site" — nurse present during all or most daytime hours |
| **CNA** | Certified Nursing Assistant | Frontline caregiver; primary hands-on care provider in most CBRFs and nursing homes |

### Payment & Funding

| Acronym | Full Name | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **HCBS** | Home and Community Based Services | Medicaid waiver program that funds care in community settings (CBRFs, RCACs) rather than nursing homes; eligibility requires facility HCBS compliance |
| **VA** | Veterans Affairs | Federal agency offering benefits (Aid & Attendance) that can offset AL costs for qualifying veterans and surviving spouses |

### Regulatory & Oversight

| Acronym | Full Name | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **DQA** | Division of Quality Assurance | Wisconsin DHS division that licenses and inspects CBRFs, RCACs, and other non-nursing-home facilities |
| **DHS** | Department of Health Services | Wisconsin state agency; parent of DQA |
| **CMS** | Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services | Federal agency; regulates SNFs; publishes inspection data on CMS Care Compare (medicare.gov) |
| **IJ** | Immediate Jeopardy | Most severe CMS deficiency classification for nursing homes; indicates a condition that has caused or is likely to cause serious injury, harm, or death |
| **SQC** | Substandard Quality of Care | CMS designation triggered by Immediate Jeopardy or certain pattern deficiencies; triggers extended survey authority and NATCEP prohibition |
| **NATCEP** | Nurse Aide Training and Competency Evaluation Program | Nursing home training program; facilities with SQC findings are barred from offering it for 2 years |
| **CCN** | CMS Certification Number | Federal identifier for Medicare/Medicaid-certified facilities; used to look up SNF inspection records on CMS Care Compare |

### Consumer Review Platforms

| Acronym | Full Name | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **APFM** | A Place for Mom | Major senior living review and referral platform; scores out of 10; reviews are verified placements |
| **MCP** | My Caring Plan | Aggregates reviews from multiple platforms; publishes Wisconsin statewide rankings out of 1,160 communities |

### Other

| Acronym | Full Name | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **WALA** | Wisconsin Assisted Living Association | Industry association; Diamond Accreditation is a higher credential than general membership |
| **BBB** | Better Business Bureau | Consumer complaint tracking organization; not a regulatory body |
| **iN2L** | It's Never 2 Late | Interactive touchscreen technology platform used for personalized resident engagement; present at Legacy of DeForest |
