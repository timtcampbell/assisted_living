## Facility Profile

- **Type:** Community-Based Residential Facility (CBRF) — Assisted Living & Memory Care
- **Capacity:** 16 private rooms (small/boutique scale)
- **Served populations:** Advanced aged; irreversible dementia/Alzheimer's
- **Staffing:** 24-hour staff; no on-site nurse (nursing services arranged via third parties)
- **Payment:** Private pay only — no Medicare or Medicaid accepted; long-term care insurance and Veterans Aid & Attendance benefits accepted
- **Pricing:** Approximately $6,000–$8,000/month (area average ~$5,240)

## What Reviewers Say

**Positives consistently cited:**
- Small size means more individualized attention ("less residents for the staff to take care of")
- Staff described as kind, attentive, knowledgeable, and cheerful
- Warm, home-like atmosphere rather than institutional feel
- Strong family communication ("Done a great job communicating to our family anything regarding our loved one's needs")
- Variety of daily activities and outings; residents described as happy and engaged
- Competitive pricing compared to Madison-area alternatives (one reviewer cited $2,000–$3,000/month savings)
- Private rooms with ADA showers, salon, cable/Wi-Fi included

**Notable quotes:**
- *"A warm homelike atmosphere where staff works hard to create a safe and loving environment."* — A Place for Mom reviewer
- *"It's a smaller place, which was ideal for him. The staff members seem good."* — Caring.com reviewer (April 2022)
- *"[Resident] seems to be happy with the meals and dining service."* — A Place for Mom reviewer

**Criticisms / caveats:**
- No negative reviews found on any public platform
- Review volume is low (4–11 depending on source), which limits statistical confidence
- No on-site nurse — a consideration for residents with higher medical needs

## Bottom Line

Beehive Homes of Mt. Horeb is a small, well-regarded memory care and assisted living facility with uniformly positive consumer reviews across all major platforms. Its My Caring Plan statewide rank (#31 out of 1,160 Wisconsin communities) is the highest of any facility in this research set, and it is the only CBRF in the set with no regulatory violations found in any public record and no management concerns raised in any review source. Its key strengths are its boutique size (16 beds), home-like culture, and high staff-to-resident ratio. The main limitations are the private-pay-only model, the absence of an on-site nurse, and relatively few public reviews. Among the nine facilities researched, Beehive is the clearest recommendation for families who do not need Medicaid coverage and whose loved one does not require a licensed nurse on-site — it outperforms every other CBRF in this set on consumer satisfaction and regulatory record while pricing in the middle of the group. Families who need Medicaid eligibility should look to [Heritage Middleton](heritage_middleton.md), [Homestead Living Waunakee](homestead_living_waunakee.md), or [Brookdale Middleton Stonefield](brookdale_middleton_stonefield.md) instead. For authoritative inspection history, contact the Wisconsin DHS at dhswebmaildqa@dhs.wisconsin.gov or reference CBRF license 0019361.
